Reading Project on Clouds & Atmospheric Dynamics

After completing my midterm exams in the fifth semester, I wanted to know more about cloud classification and observation because of my particular fascination of this atmospheric phenomenon, and before the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics course in sixth semester, I wanted to delve a bit into Atmospheric Dynamics. Seeking guidance, I approached Dr Suhas Ettamal, who recommended ‘Cloud Dynamics’ by Robert A. Houze, Jr. This book proved to be exactly what I wanted to learn.

The opening chapter of the book provided me with valuable insights into cloud classification, emphasizing the importance of assessing base altitudes and structural characteristics, familiarizing me with terms like stratus, cumulus and cirrus clouds.

Drawing on the knowledge from the chapter, I was able to analyse the cloud photographs I had taken out of sheer curiosity. All my observations and the insights I gained from the book are detailed in the report linked here.

The Second Chapter centered on diverse themes within Atmospheric Dynamics, offering a comprehensive exploration into fundamental concepts. For a detailed account of these topics, please refer to the report linked above. As I approached the sixth semester, I realized that I had acquired a desired foundation just in time. Anticipating more advanced studies in fluid mechanics from courses of Geophysical Fluid Dynamics and Physical Oceanography, I eagerly looked forward to the prospect of delving into intricate details and, hopefully, engaging in a potential research project.
